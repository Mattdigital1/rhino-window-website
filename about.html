Our story

From a one-bay garage
to the parish's
most-trusted shop.

Rhino Window Tint opened its doors in 2018 in St Amant, LA. The team had spent more than 15 combined years tinting vehicles — long enough to see the same problems pop up over and over: bubbled rear glass, purple-faded fronts, blade marks on weather seals, "lifetime warranties" that vanished when the original installer disappeared.

So we opened Rhino with one simple rule: install tint the way you'd want it on your own daily driver. Premium GeoShield film. Computer-cut precision. Real ceramic instead of "ceramic-blend" marketing fluff. A warranty that actually means something — written down, honored years later without an argument.

Years later, with hundreds of five-star reviews and a Pro Dealer credential from GeoShield, that one bay has become the most-trusted tint shop in Ascension Parish. We're still small on purpose. Every install is hand-checked under inspection lighting before we hand the keys back.

Real questions

Stuff people
always ask.

Tint laws, cure time, warranty — quick answers to the questions we hear every day. Need something we didn't cover? Just call us →

Louisiana law for passenger vehicles: front side windows must allow at least 40% VLT (Visible Light Transmittance). Rear side windows and rear window have no minimum — go as dark as 5%. Reflectivity must not exceed 20%. Same 40% rule applies to SUVs and trucks on the front sides.

Dyed is the entry-tier — looks dark, but rejects little heat and can fade purple in 3–5 years.

Carbon is mid-tier. Doesn't fade, rejects more heat than dyed, deeper black look. Solid daily-driver pick.

Ceramic is the top tier. Nano-ceramic particles reject up to 80% IR (heat) and 99% UV without going dark. Zero signal interference for phones, GPS, radar, or toll transponders. The Louisiana sun makes it a no-brainer for most of our customers.

Most full-vehicle tints take 2–3 hours in the bay. Single-window or rear-only jobs run about 45 minutes.

Initial film set is 4–6 hours. Full cure takes 3–7 days depending on humidity and temperature. Don't roll the windows down for the first 3–5 days. Slight haze, water bubbles, or moisture pockets during cure are normal — they disappear as the film dries.

Depends on the vehicle and the film tier. Rough starting points:
